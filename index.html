Auto Payment QR Placement Research

Overview

A comprehensive study on optimal QR code placement for automotive payment systems, focusing on user convenience, safety, and technical feasibility.

Research Methods

  • Contextual Inquiry with 15 drivers
  • Competitive Analysis of existing solutions
  • Heuristic Evaluation
  • Eye-tracking studies

Key Findings

Through our research, we discovered that the optimal placement for QR codes in vehicles is on the central console, angled 15° toward the driver. This placement scored highest on visibility, accessibility, and minimal driving distraction metrics.

Impact

The research findings were implemented by three major automotive manufacturers, resulting in a 23% increase in successful first-time QR scans and a 17% reduction in time spent on payment processes.

ZAP Smart Shopping App

Overview

ZAP is a smart shopping companion that helps users create shopping lists, navigate stores efficiently, and find the best deals.

Ideation Process

After identifying key pain points in the shopping experience through user interviews, I conducted several ideation workshops using techniques like crazy eights and mind mapping to generate innovative solutions.

Wireframing

I created low-fidelity wireframes to establish the information architecture and core user flows. These wireframes were tested with potential users and iterated upon based on feedback.

Outcome

The wireframes provided a solid foundation for the UI design phase, establishing clear user flows and information hierarchy that addressed the core user needs identified in research.

Midair Social Flight App

Overview

Midair is a social app that allows air travelers to connect with fellow passengers, share travel tips, and enhance their in-flight experience.

Prototyping Process

After completing the UI design, I created interactive prototypes to test the user experience and identify potential usability issues before development.

Prototype Fidelity Progression

Low-fidelity

Paper prototypes for quick validation

Mid-fidelity

Wireflow with basic interactions

High-fidelity

Fully interactive prototype with animations

User Testing Insights

The prototypes were tested with 12 frequent flyers, revealing key insights about privacy concerns and feature discoverability that were addressed in subsequent iterations.

FocusRead Browser Extension

Overview

FocusRead is a browser extension that enhances reading experience by removing distractions from web content, allowing users to focus on what matters.

Testing & Iteration Process

After developing an initial version of the extension, I conducted extensive usability testing to identify pain points and areas for improvement.

Testing Methods

  • Usability testing with 20 participants
  • A/B testing of different reading modes
  • Heatmap analysis of user interactions
  • Surveys and feedback collection

Iteration Highlights

Before

Initial design with limited customization

After

Refined design with user-requested features

Results

Through iterative testing and refinement, user satisfaction scores increased by 35%, and the average reading session duration increased by 42%.
